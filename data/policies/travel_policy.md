# Global Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy

## 1. Purpose [POL-PURP-001]
The Global Travel and Expense (T&E) Policy exists to provide clear guidelines for all employees undertaking business travel on behalf of the Company. This policy is designed to:
- Ensure safety and security for all traveling personnel.
- Standardise reimbursement procedures across global entities.
- Maintain cost-effectiveness and fiscal responsibility.
- Comply with global tax laws and audit requirements (including GDPR, SOX, and HMRC/IRS standards).
- Enable efficient automated processing through the Travel Reimbursement Approval Agent.

## 2. Scope [POL-SCOP-001]
This policy applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, contractors (where specified by contract), and interns of the Company and its subsidiaries. No individual, regardless of rank or seniority, is exempt from the documentation requirements set forth in this document.

## 3. Definitions [POL-DEF-001]
- **Business Purpose:** Any activity primary intended to further the commercial interests of the Company.
- **Commuting:** Travel between an employee's residence and their primary office location. Commuting is NOT reimbursable.
- **Daily Per-Diem:** A fixed daily allowance for meals and incidental expenses.
- **Incidental Expenses:** Small expenditures for tips, gratuities, and minor services.
- **Receipt:** An original document issued by a vendor showing the date, amount, items purchased, and tax breakdown.
- **RAG Agent:** The AI-driven Retrieval-Augmented Generation system used for initial claim adjudication.
- **Travel Status:** Commencing when an employee leaves their residence or office for a business destination and ending upon return.

## 4. Employee Eligibility [POL-ELIG-001]
To be eligible for travel reimbursement, an employee must:
1. Have an active employment status during the duration of the trip.
2. Ensure their profile in the HR Management System (HRMS) is up-to-date.
3. Obtain written or electronic Pre-Travel Authorization (PTA) from their direct manager for any trip involving overnight stay or international borders.

## 5. Business Travel Eligibility [POL-ELIG-002]
Travel is considered business-related if it meets at least one of the following criteria:
- Client meetings or sales presentations.
- Internal team summits or strategic planning sessions (approved by VP level).
- Participation in industry conferences or professional development seminars.
- On-site technical support or implementation at client locations.
- Relocation travel approved by Human Resources.
- **Exclusion:** Personal side-trips or "bleisure" (business + leisure) extensions must be explicitly separated in the claim. The company will only reimburse the business portion. [POL-ELIG-003]

## 6. Domestic Travel Policy [POL-DOM-001]
Domestic travel is defined as travel within the borders of the country where the employee's primary office is located. 
- **Standard:** Employees should use the most economical means of transport (e.g., rail or economy flights).
- **Accommodation:** Limited to standard business hotels.
- **Booking Window:** Travel must be booked at least 14 days in advance where possible to capture lower rates.

## 7. International Travel Policy [POL-INTL-001]
International travel is defined as travel across national borders. 
- **Approval:** Requires Regional Director or VP-level approval.
- **Health & Safety:** Employees must check the Global Security Portal for risk ratings before travel.
- **Class of Service:** Subject to flight duration rules (see Section 8).
- **Exchange Rates:** Claims must be made in the currency incurred, or converted using the Corporate Exchange Rate (OANDA) for the date of the transaction. [POL-INTL-002]

## 8. Flight Policy [POL-FLIGHT-001]
- **Class of Travel (Economy):** All domestic flights and international flights under 6 hours of total air time must be booked in Economy or Premium Economy.
- **Class of Travel (Business):** Business class is permitted only for international flights with a continuous duration exceeding 8 hours (one-way), or where a medical exemption is on file. [POL-FLIGHT-002]
- **Upgrades:** Complimentary upgrades may be kept by the employee. Paid upgrades for personal comfort are the responsibility of the employee.
- **Ancillary Fees:** Reasonable baggage fees (1 checked bag) and seat selection for long-haul flights are reimbursable. In-flight Wi-Fi is only reimbursable if necessary for immediate work. [POL-FLIGHT-003]


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Approve:** Standard Economy/Premium class for flights < 8 hours; Business class for flights > 8 hours with VP approval.
*   **Partial Approval:** N/A (Flight fares are usually all-or-nothing).
*   **Manual Review:** Economy class booked < 14 days without justification; Business class for flights > 8 hours but missing VP documentation.
*   **Reject:** First class tickets; Business class for short-haul (< 8 hours) without medical exemption.


## 9. Hotel Accommodation Policy [POL-HOTEL-001]

### 9.1 Domestic Hotel Accommodation (India) [POL-HOTEL-001A]
Employees traveling within India are eligible for accommodation in business-grade hotels based on city classification:
*   **Tier 1 Indian Cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Hyderabad):** Max ₹8,000/night (excluding taxes).
*   **Tier 2 Indian Cities (Pune, Chennai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad):** Max ₹6,000/night (excluding taxes).
*   **Tier 3 Indian Cities (All other locations):** Max ₹4,500/night (excluding taxes).

### 9.2 International Hotel Accommodation [POL-HOTEL-001B]
Employees traveling outside of India must adhere to the following limits:
*   **Tier 1 Global Cities (London, NY, SF, Tokyo, Zurich):** Max $350/night (excluding taxes).
*   **Tier 2 Global Cities (Berlin, Madrid, Sydney, Dubai):** Max $250/night (excluding taxes).
*   **Tier 3 Global Cities (All other international locations):** Max $175/night (excluding taxes).

### 9.3 Standard Accomodation Rules [POL-HOTEL-002]
*   **Standard Room:** Employees are entitled to a standard single room. Upgrades to suites or luxury rooms are non-reimbursable unless the standard room is unavailable and prior manager approval is obtained.
*   **Incidental Charges:** Personal room service, mini-bar, in-room movies, and spa services are non-reimbursable [POL-REJECT-001A].
*   **Booking:** All bookings must be made through the Corporate Booking Tool (CBT).
## 10. Meal Reimbursement Policy [POL-MEAL-001]
Employees may choose between **Actual Expenses** (requires receipts) or **Per-Diem** (does not require receipts for small amounts, depending on local tax law). Choice must be consistent for the entire trip.
- **Individual Meal Limits (Actuals):**
  - International Breakfast: up to $25 / Domestic: ₹500
  - International Lunch: up to $40 / Domestic: ₹800
  - International Dinner: up to $75 / Domestic: ₹1200
- **Business Meals with Clients:** Must include names of attendees and the specific business purpose. Limits are $150 per person inclusive of tax and gratuity. [POL-MEAL-002]
- **Prohibited Items:** Alcohol is only reimbursable during client dinners with prior VP approval. Personal alcohol consumption is never reimbursable. [POL-MEAL-003]


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Approve:** Items within meal-specific limits ($25/$40/$75); Proper itemization provided.
*   **Partial Approval:** Receipt total exceeds limit (approve up to max limit only).
*   **Manual Review:** Group dinner (>3 people) without list of attendees; Alcohol line items detected in a standard meal.
*   **Reject:** Personal alcohol consumption; "Groceries" or "Supermarket" claims during travel; Claims with no itemized receipt.

## 11. Local Transportation Policy [POL-TRANS-001]
- **Taxis and Rideshares (Uber/Lyft):** Reimbursable for transport between airport/station and hotel, or hotel and client office. Premium/Luxury tiers (Uber Black, etc.) are NOT reimbursable unless it is the only option available.
- **Public Transport:** Use of subways, buses, and trains is strongly encouraged and fully reimbursable.
- **Airport Parking:** Standard long-term parking is preferred. Valet parking is not reimbursable.


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Approve:** Standard Uber/Lyft or Public Transit; Mileage following standard routes.
*   **Partial Approval:** Mileage reported exceeds route distance by 10-15%.
*   **Manual Review:** Luxury car tiers (Uber Black/6-star) selected; Taxi trips > $100.
*   **Reject:** Traffic/Parking fines; Mileage for personal errands; Excess mileage > 15% without justification.

## 12. Personal Vehicle Reimbursement [POL-VEH-001]
Authorized use of a personal vehicle for business travel is reimbursable on a per-mile/per-km basis. 
- **Insurance:** Employees must maintain minimum liability insurance as required by law. The company is not liable for damage to personal vehicles.
- **Approval:** Only permitted if it is more cost-effective than air or rail travel or if no other options exist.

## 13. Mileage Reimbursement [POL-VEH-002]
- **Rates:** Reimbursed at the current government-mandated rate (e.g., IRS standard mileage rate in the US).
- **Calculation:** Distance must be calculated using Google Maps (shortest route). Commuting distance must be subtracted from the total.
- **Documentation:** Log of dates, origins, destinations, and business purposes must be attached. [POL-VEH-003]

## 14. Parking, Fuel and Toll Policy [POL-PARK-003]
- **Fuel:** Only reimbursable for rental cars. Personal vehicle fuel is covered by the mileage rate.
- **Tolls:** Reimbursable with proof of payment (electronic toll tag statements or receipts).
- **Parking Fines:** Traffic violations, speeding tickets, and parking fines are the sole responsibility of the employee and will NEVER be reimbursed. [POL-TLL-002]

## 15. Internet & Communication Expenses [POL-COMM-001]
- **Home/Office:** Regular home internet subscriptions are not reimbursable under the travel policy.
- **Travel Wi-Fi:** Hotel and In-flight Wi-Fi for business tasks is permitted if not included in the room rate.
- **Mobile Data:** International roaming packs must be pre-approved or activated through the corporate mobile plan. Emergency roaming charges require manual justification.

## 16. Laundry Expenses [POL-LAUND-001]
- **Eligibility:** Reimbursable for trips exceeding 5 consecutive business days.
- **Limit:** Up to $30 per week for cleaning/pressing. Express/Overnight services are excluded without justification.

## 17. Conference & Training Expenses [POL-CONF-001]
- **Registration:** Should be paid via Corporate Credit Card. If paid personally, a copy of the agenda and receipt is required.
- **Travel:** Only the dates of the conference plus one travel day before and after are reimbursable.

## 18. Visa & Immigration Expenses [POL-VISA-001]
- **Direct Costs:** Visa fees, required photographs, and courier fees for business visas are fully reimbursable.
- **Passports:** Issuance or renewal of a personal passport is an employee's personal expense.
- **Expedited Services:** Reimbursable only if the travel request was made last-minute by the Company.

## 19. Foreign Currency & Exchange Rate Rules [POL-CURR-001]
- **Conversion:** When using a personal card, the expense must be claimed in the local currency of the transaction. The system will use the exchange rate from the date of the receipt.
- **Fees:** Foreign transaction fees applied by the employee's bank are reimbursable if proof of fee is provided via bank statement.

## 20. Receipt Requirements [POL-RECEIPT-001]
- **Threshold:** ALL expenses regardless of amount require an itemised receipt. Credit card slips or bank statements alone are insufficient for items >$25.
- **Digital Copies:** Clear, legible photos of receipts are accepted. Original paper receipts should be kept for 3 months following the claim.
- **Itemisation:** Receipts must show individual items purchased, especially for restaurant bills (to audit for alcohol).


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Approve:** Clear digital image; Mandatory fields (Date, Vendor, Amount, Tax) matched with claim.
*   **Partial Approval:** N/A.
*   **Manual Review:** Blurry image; Missing Tax ID/GST Number; Scanned handwritten note.
*   **Reject:** Total black-of-receipt image; Receipt date does not match travel dates; Forged or altered receipt data.

## 21. Missing Receipt Policy [POL-RECEIPT-003]
If a receipt is lost or not provided by the vendor:
1. The employee must attempt to obtain a duplicate.
2. If unsuccessful, a "Missing Receipt Declaration" form must be signed by the Manager and Director.
3. Claims without receipts for amounts > $50 will be flagged for **Manual Review**. [POL-MANUAL-001]

## 22. Duplicate Claim Policy [POL-DUP-001]

### 22.2 Advanced Duplicate Detection Multi-Vector Analysis [POL-DUP-003]
The AI Agent performs a 7-point audit to detect potential fraud or double-submissions:
1.  **Receipt ID/Invoice Number:** Mandatory exact match check across the 7-year history.
2.  **Vendor Context:** Compares Merchant names for common aliases (e.g., "Starbucks" vs "SBUX").
3.  **Amount & Currency:** Exact matching of total values.
4.  **Transaction Date:** Matches within a +/- 24-hour window for timezone shifts.
5.  **Receipt Hash Mapping:** Digital fingerprinting of the uploaded file to prevent re-uploading the same image.
6.  **OCR Similarity:** Comparing itemized line items to ensure different receipts aren't being used for the same vendor.
7.  **Merchant Similarity:** Detecting if two different receipts were generated for the same timestamp at the same GPS location.

- **Detection:** The automated system cross-references vendor names, dates, and amounts against previous claims.
- **Rule:** Submission of the same receipt twice (even across different trips) is strictly prohibited. If detected, the claim is flagged for **Manual Review**. [POL-DUP-002]


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Approve:** No matching Hash, ID, or Date/Vendor combination found in history.
*   **Partial Approval:** N/A.
*   **Manual Review:** High similarity score in OCR text but different Merchant; Potential overlap with another employee on the same trip.
*   **Reject:** Exact Receipt ID or Invoice Number match; Hash collision with previous submission ($0 approved).

## 23. Fraud Prevention & Compliance [POL-FRAUD-001]
- **Definition of Fraud:** False reporting of mileage, altering receipts, claiming personal items as business, or collusion with vendors.
- **Consequences:** Fraudulent claims result in immediate rejection, retrieval of funds, and disciplinary action up to termination.
- **Internal Audit:** 10% of all "Approved" claims are subject to random post-payment manual audit. [POL-FRAUD-002]

## 24. Spending Limits [POL-LIM-001]
Global limits are set relative to the cost of living in the destination country. 
- **Exceeding Limits:** Any expense exceeding the category limit by >20% will trigger a **Partial Approval** or **Manual Review**. [POL-PARTIAL-001]

## 25. Daily Per-Diem Rules [POL-PERDIEM-001]
- **Rate:** Based on the GSA (US) or local government (EU/APAC) rates for the city.
- **Deductions:** If a meal is provided for free (e.g., conference lunch, hotel breakfast), the per-diem must be reduced accordingly (Breakfast: 20%, Lunch: 30%, Dinner: 50%).

## 26. Weekend Stay Policy [POL-WKND-001]
- **Requirement:** Weekend stays are only reimbursable if the business trip spans across multiple weeks or if staying over a Saturday night results in a flight cost saving greater than the cost of the hotel and meals. 
- **Manual Review:** All weekend hotel nights require a written justification of the business necessity. [POL-MANUAL-002]

## 27. Family Travel Restrictions [POL-FAM-001]
- **Companion Travel:** Spouses, partners, and family members may accompany an employee, but ALL incremental costs (higher room rate, meals, flights) must be paid for by the employee.
- **Documentation:** The employee must provide a "shadow" quote of what the cost would have been for a single traveler to prove no extra cost was incurred by the Company. [POL-FAM-002]

### 24.1 Regional Tier Classification [POL-LIM-002]
The Company categorises global destinations into tiers to determine spending limits. These tiers are reviewed by the Global Procurement Office every six months based on the Mercer Cost of Living Index.

#### Tier 1: High Cost (Global Hubs)
- **Locations:** New York City, San Francisco, London, Paris, Zurich, Geneva, Hong Kong, Singapore, Tokyo, Dubai.
- **Hotel Limit:** Up to $350 USD (or equivalent) per night excluding taxes.
- **Meal Allowance (Daily):** $150 USD.
- **Local Transport Limit:** $80 USD per day.

#### Tier 2: Moderate Cost (Regional Cities)
- **Locations:** Chicago, Los Angeles, Berlin, Madrid, Sydney, Toronto, Seoul, Mumbai.
- **Hotel Limit:** Up to $250 USD per night excluding taxes.
- **Meal Allowance (Daily):** $110 USD.
- **Local Transport Limit:** $50 USD per day.

#### Tier 3: Standard Cost (Other Locations)
- **Locations:** Non-metropolitan areas and secondary cities.
- **Hotel Limit:** Up to $175 USD per night excluding taxes.
- **Meal Allowance (Daily):** $85 USD.
- **Local Transport Limit:** $35 USD per day.

### 24.2 Seasonal Adjustments [POL-LIM-003]
During peak-demand periods (e.g., World Economic Forum in Davos, CES in Las Vegas, major industry trade shows), a temporary limit increase of 50% is automatically applied if the "Official Event Flag" is enabled in the RAG Agent. If not enabled, the claim will require **Manual Review** [POL-MANUAL-006].

### 24.3 Excess Expenditure Protocol [POL-LIM-004]
When an employee incurs an expense exceeding the tier-based limit by less than 10%, it may be auto-approved if the manager provides a justification note. Expenses exceeding limits by >10% but <50% are subject to **Partial Approval** [POL-PARTIAL-004], where the employee is paid only up to the limit plus 10%. Expenses >50% over the limit are automatically flagged for **Manager and Finance Audit** [POL-AUDIT-002].

## 28. Advance Payments [POL-ADV-001]
- **Availability:** Travel advances are only available for employees without a Corporate Credit Card and for trips lasting >10 days. 
- **Request:** Must be submitted at least 14 days prior to travel.
- **Settlement:** Advances must be cleared within 15 days of return. New advances will not be issued if previous ones are outstanding.

## 29. Claim Submission Timeline [POL-TIME-001]
- **Deadline:** Claims must be submitted within 30 calendar days of the trip's end date.
- **Monthly Expenses:** For ongoing travel, claims should be submitted monthly.
- **End of Year:** All expenses incurred in December must be submitted by January 5th to meet fiscal year-end closing requirements. [POL-TIME-002]

## 30. Late Submission Rules [POL-TIME-003]
- **31-60 Days:** Requires written justification and Manager approval.
- **61-90 Days:** Reimbursed only as a one-time exception with VP approval.
- **>90 Days:** The claim will be automatically **Rejected**. [POL-REJECT-001]

## 31. Partial Approval Rules [POL-PARTIAL-002]
Partial approval occurs when a portion of the claim is valid but another portion violates policy.
1. **Hotel Overages:** If a hotel costs $400/night but the limit is $300, only $300 will be approved.
2. **Personal Upkeep:** If a receipt includes both business meals and a personal magazine, the magazine cost will be deducted.
3. **Missing Receipt items:** In a multi-item claim, items without receipts may be denied while others are approved.
4. **Mileage Excess:** If the reported distance exceeds the Google Maps route by >15%, only the standard route distance is approved. [POL-PARTIAL-003]

## 32. Auto Approval Rules [POL-AUTO-001]
The Approval Agent will automatically approve claims that meet ALL of the following:
- All receipts are present and match the claim amounts exactly.
- All items are within the specified financial limits for the city/tier.
- The claim is submitted within 30 days.
- The employee has a valid pre-travel authorization on file.
- Total claim value is < $2,000 (Domestic) or < $5,000 (International). [POL-AUTO-002]

## 33. Manual Review Conditions [POL-MANUAL-003]
A claim will be sent to the Finance Department for manual review if:
- Total claim value exceeds the Auto-Approval threshold.
- Currency conversion manually entered differs from the bank rate by >5%.
- "Missing Receipt Declaration" is used for any item >$50. [POL-MANUAL-004]
- The AI detects a potential duplicate from over 6 months ago.
- The hotel stay includes dates labeled as "Saturday" or "Sunday" without a corresponding business meeting on those days.
- Flights are in Business/First class without an attached VP-approval PDF.
- The merchant name on the receipt is a known "High Risk" vendor (e.g., casinos, jewelry stores). [POL-MANUAL-005]


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Action:** Trigger `Manual_Escalation_Workflow`.
*   **Notification:** Alert Finance Auditor and Manager.
*   **Threshold:** Any claim with a Confidence Score < 75% requires human sign-off.

## 34. Claim Rejection Rules [POL-REJECT-002]
The following claims will be rejected by the system or finance team:
- **Personal Purchases:** Clothing, luggage, toiletries, or home office furniture.
- **Prohibited Items:** Alcohol (unless client-facing), movies, mini-bar, tobacco, or legal fines. [POL-REJECT-003]
- **Late Submissions:** Claims over 90 days old without an executive override.
- **Duplicates:** If the same receipt has already been paid.
- **Insufficient Evidence:** Hand-written notes that do not qualify as legal receipts.
- **Ineligible Personnel:** Claims submitted on behalf of family members or non-employees.


### AI Decision Impact
*   **Action:** Status updated to `Final_Rejected`.
*   **Notification:** Send rejection email to employee citing specific Policy ID and Reason Code.
*   **Appeal:** Employee may resubmit with corrected information if rejection was due to "Incomplete Evidence".

## 35. Exception Handling [POL-EXC-001]
Exceptions are granted only for:
- Medical emergencies during travel.
- Last-minute flight changes due to business priority (requires VP confirmation).
- Lack of hotel availability in a tier-1 city forcing a higher rate.
- Exception requests must be filed as a separate "Case" with the Finance team AFTER the claim is flagged for review.

### 33.1 Advanced Manual Review Situations [POL-MANUAL-007]
The AI Approval Agent is programmed to escalate complex scenarios to a Human Auditor under these specific conditions:
- **Merchant Category Mismatch:** A receipt is uploaded for a "Consultancy Service" but the traveler's role is not authorised for procurement.
- **Location Anomaly:** A meal receipt is submitted from a city or country that is not on the traveler's approved itinerary or flight path.
- **Time/Date Drift:** Hotel charges appearing on the statement for days prior to the approved trip start date or after the trip end date.
- **Group Expenses:** A single restaurant receipt for >$500 where the "Attendee List" field is left blank.
- **Unusual Currency Fluctuations:** If the currency used in the claim is subject to hyper-inflation (defined as >5% daily volatility), the system requires a manual check of the actual bank statement rate. [POL-MANUAL-008]

### 34.1 Explicit Rejection Scenarios (The Zero-Tolerance List) [POL-REJECT-004]
To maintain corporate compliance (SOX), the following items are strictly non-reimbursable and will result in immediate claim rejection:
1. **Personal Grooming:** Haircuts, massages, manicures, or luxury spa treatments even if performed within the business hotel.
2. **Recreational Electronics:** Chargers, headphones, or power banks purchased at the airport for personal convenience.
3. **In-Flight Comfort:** Fees for "Extra Legroom" in Economy class without a medical certificate.
4. **Subscription Services:** Monthly fees for Netflix, Spotify, or LinkedIn Premium unless specifically required by the Marketing department.
5. **Membership Fees:** Airport lounge memberships (Priority Pass, etc.) are personal expenses. The Company provides lounge access only to Business Class travelers on long-haul flights. [POL-REJECT-005]
6. **Pet Care:** Kennel fees, pet-sitting, or "Pet Travel" additions to flights.
7. **Home Maintenance:** While the employee is away, no reimbursement is provided for lawn care, home security, or mail holding services.
8. **Automobile Maintenance:** Repair costs for personal vehicles damaged during a business trip. This is covered by the employee’s personal insurance. [POL-REJECT-006]
9. **Luxury Services:** Valet parking when self-parking was available, or concierge "Fast Track" services at immigration.

## Appendix A: Corporate Compliance Audit Framework [POL-AUDIT-003]

To ensure adherence to international anti-corruption laws (such as the US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act - FCPA and the UK Bribery Act), all travel and expense activities are monitored via the following compliance framework:

2. **Itemisation:** Receipts that do not separate VAT/sales tax will be rejected or significantly delayed, as they cannot be processed through the tax recovery system. [POL-REJECT-007]
3. **Training:** Employees traveling to high-VAT regions are expected to complete the 15-minute "Global Tax Compliance" module on the internal portal.

## Appendix B: Detailed Auto-Approval Logic for the AI Agent [POL-AUTO-003]

For a claim to be processed without human intervention, the system (LangGraph-based agent) evaluates the 'Confidence Score' based on:
- **Receipt OCR Accuracy:** Must be >95% (as determined by the Google Gemini/Vision tool).
- **Date Alignment:** Expense dates must fall within Departure Date (D) and Arrival Date (A). Expenses on D-1 or A+1 are allowed only for airport parking or taxi.
- **Merchant Verification:** The merchant name must be verifiable against the corporate database or standard SIC (Standard Industrial Classification) codes.
- **Semantic Check:** The "Statement of Business Purpose" field must contain at least 15 words and include non-generic keywords (e.g., specific project names or client IDs). [POL-AUTO-004]

## 58. Claim Processing Lifecycle [POL-FLOW-001]
NovaTech Solutions utilizes an automated AI-driven workflow for expense processing:

1.  **Employee Submission:** Claim is submitted via the Mobility App.
2.  **Claim Validation:** System checks if the claim is within the 30-day window.
3.  **Policy Retrieval (RAG):** AI Agent retrieves the relevant policy sections (Flight, Hotel, etc.).
4.  **Receipt Verification (OCR):** Receipt tools perform extraction and confidence scoring.
5.  **Limit Validation:** AI checks against the **Employee Role Matrix** and city tiers.
6.  **Duplicate Check:** Hash and vector similarity analysis is performed.
7.  **Approval Threshold Check:** Automated check to see if Auto-Approval is possible.
8.  **LLM Reasoning:** The agent analyzes all data points to form a decision.
9.  **AI Decision:** Agent outputs structured JSON (Approve/Reject/Review).
10. **Manager Approval:** Required if AI recommends "Partial Approval" or if role limits are exceeded.
11. **Finance Review:** Required for high-value claims (>₹50,000 / $5,000) or Fraud flags.

## Appendix H: Receipt Validation Rules for RAG OCR [POL-RECEIPT-004]
For the LangChain OCR tool to process a receipt as "Verified", it must extract and validate the following fields:
1.  **Receipt/Invoice Number:** Search for strings like "Inv #", "Transaction ID", "Bill No".
2.  **Vendor Identity:** Match Merchant name with Global Vendor Database.
3.  **Tax Information:** Extract Tax Grouping (VAT/GST/JCT). Required for any expense > $100.
4.  **Date/Time:** Must fall within the authorized trip period.
5.  **Currency Code:** Recognize ISO symbols ($, £, €, ¥, ₹).
6.  **Line Items:** Every row must be parsed to detect "Prohibited Keywords" (Vodka, Spa, Gift, Personal).
7.  **Payment Method:** Recognition of "Cash", "Visa ****", "Corporate Amex".

## Appendix K: OCR Confidence Rules [POL-OCR-001]
The AI Agent utilizes an Optical Character Recognition (OCR) tool to validate receipt authenticity.

*   **95–100% Confidence:** Receipt is accepted as high fidelity. Fields are auto-mapped.
*   **90–94% Confidence:** System issues a 'Warning' flag. AI must check logic against bank statements if available.
*   **70–89% Confidence:** **Manual Review** required. The visual quality is insufficient for final adjudication.
*   **Below 70% Confidence:** **Reject OCR Result**. The employee must re-upload a clearer image.

## Appendix L: Duplicate Detection Similarity Rules [POL-DUP-004]
Similarity for duplicate detection is calculated based on a weighted average of: Receipt ID, Invoice Number, Vendor Name, Date, Amount, Currency, OCR Text Similarity, Receipt Hash, and Merchant Similarity.

*   **95–100% Similarity:** **Reject**. This is a confirmed duplicate claim (RC002).
*   **80–94% Similarity:** **Manual Review**. Potential split invoice or same-day merchant visit.
*   **60–79% Similarity:** **Warning**. AI notifies the employee to verify if they haven't submitted this before.
*   **Below 60% Similarity:** Unique Receipt. Valid for standard processing.

## Appendix Q: AI Agent Architecture [POL-AGENT-003]

### Purpose

This Travel & Expense Reimbursement Policy is designed to serve as the primary knowledge base for the **NovaTech AI Travel Reimbursement Approval Agent**.

The AI Agent uses this policy to evaluate employee reimbursement claims consistently, transparently, and in accordance with enterprise travel policies.

---

### Technology Stack

The AI Agent is built using:

- **LangGraph** – Orchestrates the end-to-end reimbursement workflow.
- **LangChain** – Provides document retrieval and tool integration.
- **Google Gemini** – Performs reasoning and generates policy-based decisions.
- **ChromaDB** – Stores vector embeddings of this policy for semantic retrieval.
- **Python** – Implements the business logic and AI workflow.
- **Streamlit** – Provides the user interface for claim submission and review.

---

### AI Processing Workflow

Every reimbursement claim follows the workflow below:

1. Employee submits a reimbursement claim.
2. The AI Agent validates the claim structure.
3. Relevant policy sections are retrieved from ChromaDB using semantic search.
4. The AI Agent invokes the required validation tools:
   - Policy Retriever
   - Receipt Checker
   - Expense Limit Checker
   - Duplicate Checker
   - Approval Checker
   - Output Validator
5. Google Gemini analyzes the retrieved policy context and tool outputs.
6. The AI Agent determines one of the following decisions:
   - Approve
   - Partially Approve
   - Reject
   - Manual Review
7. A structured JSON response is generated containing:
   - Decision
   - Approved Amount
   - Rejected Amount
   - Policy References
   - Reason Codes
   - Confidence Score
   - Audit Trail

---

### Knowledge Retrieval

This policy document is indexed into a vector database.

During claim processing, only the relevant policy sections are retrieved through semantic search. The retrieved content is supplied to the Large Language Model to ensure that every reimbursement decision is based on the official company policy rather than the model's internal knowledge.

---

### Explainability

Every AI-generated recommendation must include:

- Policy Reference IDs
- Reason Codes
- Confidence Score
- Audit Trail
- Human-readable Explanation

This ensures transparency, traceability, and compliance with enterprise governance standards.

---

### Human Oversight

The AI Agent provides reimbursement recommendations based on this policy.

The Finance Department retains final approval authority for:

- Fraud investigations
- Policy exceptions
- Executive travel
- Legal disputes
- Regulatory compliance cases
- Manual Review scenarios
